Meet Bao Bao, the Giant Panda

Bao Bao is a female giant panda cub who lives at the National Zoo in Washington D.C. She comes from the family of bears.

Bao Bao was born on August 23, 2013, at the National Zoo in Washington D.C. She is the cub of Mey Xiang (mother) and Tian Tian (father).

She was named after 100 days after her birth while her birth and naming ceremony are part of China's panda diplomacy and she wil be sent to China when she is 4 years old.

As of April 21, 2014, Bao Bao, while still spending a lot of time in the den, enjoys climbing in trees, despite a few falls, where she can be spotted by the still-heavy crowds.

She has grown to like sweet potatoes and fruit-sicles, which Mei (her mother) sometimes helps her finish.

Bao Bao and other Pandas usually live in bamboo forests where they feed on bamboo in central China while in the wild.

They used to live on lower ground, however because so many people started living in those areas, they were pushed out to move to higher ground, in the mountains of China.

How can we help?

Building paths, will connect all of the separate forests that the pandas live in.

To do this, some farmland will have to be returned to nature.

This means, it will be difficult to save the Giant Panda if people don't stop developing.

What is being done to help?

Wildlife reserves have been set up in different parts of China to help pandas such Bao Bao, that ensure the pandas have a home, and are being cared for to make sure they can survive in the wild.
